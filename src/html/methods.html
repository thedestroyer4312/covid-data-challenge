Methods

Here are the methods used to calculate risk scores found in the quiz.

Age: Age risk level was determined by taking the “percentage” value and multiplying it by 4.4 to scale it from 0-100. The data was taken from the following dataset: HERE

Race: Race risk level was determined by taking the “percentage” value and multiplying it by 2.4 to scale it from 0-100. The data was taken from the following dataset: HERE

Sex: Gender risk level was taken from the follow dataset: HERE

Location: Location risk level was determined by taking the “percentage of national cases” and multiplying it by 6 to scale it from 0-100. This data can be found at our github repo HERE

Occupation/Jobs: Risk Level was determined by each occupation ranking from 1-100 on risk of getting COVID-19. Similar occupations were averaged together to form categories, and all other jobs not not mentioned are assumed to be an average level of 23.
The data was taken from the following dataset: HERE

Activity risk: Activities were categorized based on a risk from 1-5, then expanded to fit the scale of 1-100. These activities were categorized based on data from the following website. HERE
The user checked the box next to each activity they participated in the past two weeks, and the activity risks were averaged together to form a score of the user’s general activity risk.

Mask wearing: Mask risk level was calculated based on data from the following website that stated the rate of COVID-19 transmission based on whether or not an individual wore a mask. HERE

Social Distancing: Social Distancing risk level was calculated based on distance. For each meter of distancing between two people was a range (from low to high) of risk that the individual would have. The data was pulled from the following website. HERE
The user answered based on average distance from other people, and each distance is correlated to a range of risk.
