Methods

Here are the methods used to calculate risk scores found in the quiz.

Age: Age risk level was determined by taking the “percentage” value and multiplying it by 4.4 to scale it from 0-100. The data was taken from the following dataset:

Race: Race risk level was determined by taking the “percentage” value and multiplying it by 2.4 to scale it from 0-100. The data was taken from the following dataset:

Location: Location risk level was determined by taking the “percentage of national cases” and multiplying it by 6 to scale it from 0-100. The data was taken from the following dataset:

Occupation/Jobs: Risk Level was determined by each occupation ranking from 1-100 on risk of getting COVID-19. The data was taken from the following dataset: HERE

Activity risk: Activities were categorized based on a risk from 1-5, then expanded to fit the scale of 1-100. These activities were categorized based on data from the following website. HERE

Mask wearing: Mask risk level was calculated based on data from the following website that stated the rate of COVID-19 transmission based on whether or not an individual wore a mask. HERE
